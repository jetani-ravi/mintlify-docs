## Overview

When a lead arrives from [Google or Meta Ads](/channels/advertising), EzyText
records where they came from. You can see this on each contact, and roll it up
across your whole list on the **Lead Sources** and **Campaign Performance** pages.

## On a contact

Open any ad-sourced contact and look for the **"Where this contact came from"**
card. It shows:

- **Platform** — Google Ads or Meta Ads.
- **Campaign** — the campaign that first brought them in.
- **Lead form** — the form they submitted.
- **First contact** — when they first arrived.
- **Most recent campaign** — the latest campaign touch.

<Note>
  EzyText resolves the human-readable campaign and lead-form **names** — you won't
  see opaque IDs. Rows are hidden until the name is available.
</Note>

## Lead Sources page

**Advertising → Lead Sources** shows where **all** your contacts come from — not
just ads — as a donut and a table of source, new contacts, and % of total. Sources
include CSV Import, Manual, Web Form, Text Link, Inbound SMS, Google Ads, and Meta
Ads.

## Campaign Performance page

**Advertising → Campaign Performance** turns ad activity into outcomes:

- **KPIs** — New Contacts, Appointments Booked, Qualified Leads, New Customers, and
  Conversion Rate.
- **Funnel** — Contacts → Engaged → Qualified → Appointment Booked → Customers.
- **Campaign table** — per-campaign contacts, appointments, qualified, customers,
  and conversion rate.

Filter by period (last 7/30 days, this/last month), custom date range, and platform.

## FAQ

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Does EzyText track conversions back to Google/Meta?">
    Attribution here is inbound — it traces contacts and their pipeline progress to
    an ad source. Sending conversions back to the ad platforms isn't available yet.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Why is the campaign name missing on a contact?">
    The campaign or lead form hasn't finished syncing, or the name isn't available
    yet. EzyText hides the row rather than show a raw ID.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="What's first-touch vs most-recent?">
    First contact/campaign is where they entered; most-recent campaign is the latest
    ad touch — useful when a contact engages with several campaigns.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>
