## Overview

Availability schedules define when people can book you. Each
[meeting type](/scheduling/meeting-types) uses a schedule, so slots only appear
during your bookable hours. Manage schedules under **Availability**
(`/availability`).

<Info>
  **Difficulty:** Beginner
</Info>

## Set your weekly hours

<Steps>
  <Step title="Pick or create a schedule">
    Choose an existing schedule (one is marked **Default**) or create a new one.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Set the timezone">
    Weekly hours are wall-clock times in the schedule's **timezone**.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Toggle days and set ranges">
    Turn each day on/off. For enabled days, set one or more time ranges (for example,
    9:00–12:00 and 13:00–17:00). Use **Copy to…** to reuse a day's hours on others.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Date overrides

Add **overrides** for specific dates — mark a day **unavailable** (holidays) or set
custom hours just for those dates. Overrides take priority over your weekly hours.

## Multiple schedules

Create several named schedules (for example, "Sales hours" and "Support hours") and
assign different ones to different meeting types. Meeting types using a deleted
schedule fall back to the default.

## FAQ

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Why don't external calendar events block my slots?">
    They can — connect a [calendar](/scheduling/calendar-integrations) and mark it
    "Check for conflicts" so busy times remove slots automatically.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Can different meeting types have different hours?">
    Yes — create multiple schedules and pick one per meeting type.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## Troubleshooting

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="'Time ranges overlap' / 'End must be after start'">
    Fix the day's ranges so they don't overlap and each end time is after its start.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="No slots show on my booking page">
    Confirm the schedule has enabled days with hours, the timezone is correct, and no
    override marks the date unavailable.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>
