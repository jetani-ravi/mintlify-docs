## Overview

Tickets track work items and support requests within your team — each with a
status, priority, assignee, comments, and an activity log. Open them from
**Tickets** in the sidebar (`/tickets`).

<Info>
  **Difficulty:** Beginner
</Info>

<Note>
  Tickets are standalone team tasks. They aren't linked to inbox conversations or
  contacts — they reference the teammates assigned to them.
</Note>

## Create a ticket

<Steps>
  <Step title="Click Create">
    On the Tickets page, click **Create**.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Fill in the details">
    Enter a **Subject** (required), optional **Description**, a **Priority**, an
    optional **Due** date, an **Assignee**, and **Tags**.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Save">
    New tickets start as **Open**, numbered `TCK-123`.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Views

Switch between a **List** and a **Board** (Kanban, one column per status). Search by
subject, description, or ticket number, and filter by status, priority, assignee,
tags, due date, or overdue.

## Statuses and priorities

<Tabs>
  <Tab title="Status">
    **Open → In progress → Resolved → Closed**, plus **Reopened**. Change status from
    the ticket sidebar — only valid next states are offered.
  </Tab>
  <Tab title="Priority">
    **Low**, **Medium**, **High**, **Urgent**. A ticket past its due date (and not
    resolved/closed) is flagged **overdue**.
  </Tab>
</Tabs>

## Work a ticket

Open a ticket to:

- **Comment** — discuss with your team; edit or delete your own comments.
- **Change** status, priority, assignee, due date, and tags from the sidebar.
- **Attach** files (up to 25 MB each).
- Review the **Activity** timeline of every change.

## FAQ

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Can I link a ticket to a conversation or contact?">
    Not currently — tickets are standalone team tasks referencing assignees and
    reporters.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Who is the reporter?">
    The person who created the ticket, set automatically.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Can I reopen a closed ticket?">
    Yes — closed and resolved tickets can be **Reopened**.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## Troubleshooting

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="I can't change a ticket to a certain status">
    Status changes follow a workflow (for example, you resolve before you close).
    Only allowed next statuses appear in the dropdown.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>
