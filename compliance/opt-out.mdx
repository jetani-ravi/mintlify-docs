## Overview

When someone replies **STOP** (or another opt-out keyword), they must stop
receiving your messages — it's a legal requirement and a carrier rule. EzyText
detects opt-out keywords on every inbound message, flags the contact, and excludes
them from all future campaigns automatically.

You manage opt-out keywords and the optional confirmation reply at **Settings →
Opt-out** (the page shows Opt-out and [Opt-in](/compliance/opt-in) side by side).

## How opt-out detection works

<Steps>
  <Step title="A contact replies with a keyword">
    EzyText checks each inbound message against your opt-out keywords **before** any
    other processing (so `STOP` in reply to a poll is treated as an opt-out, not a vote).
  </Step>
  <Step title="The keyword is matched">
    Matching is **case-insensitive** and **anchored to the start** of the message,
    matching whole words. `STOP`, `stop`, `STOP.`, and `STOP now` all opt out;
    `STOPOVER` does not.
  </Step>
  <Step title="The contact is opted out">
    EzyText marks the contact as opted out, records an **Opted out** entry on their
    activity timeline (with the keyword and message), and removes them from future
    campaign audiences. Repeated STOPs are a no-op.
  </Step>
  <Step title="An optional confirmation is sent">
    If you've configured an auto-reply message, EzyText sends it (see limitations
    with provider-reserved keywords below).
  </Step>
</Steps>

### Default keywords

Every workspace starts with the carrier-standard opt-out keywords:

```
STOP, STOPALL, STOP ALL, UNSUBSCRIBE, CANCEL, END, QUIT
```

## Configure opt-out

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open opt-out settings">
    Go to **Settings → Opt-out**. The left column is **Opt-Out Settings**.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Manage keywords">
    Under **Keywords**, type a keyword and press **Enter** (or click **Add**) to add
    a custom one such as `leave` or `optout`. Edit or delete any keyword inline. You
    can have up to **75 keywords** per workspace.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Set an auto-reply (optional)">
    Under **Auto-Reply Message**, enter an optional confirmation (up to **1,600
    characters**) that's sent when someone opts out, then click **Save**. Leave it
    blank to send nothing extra.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Provider-reserved keywords

Carriers and providers reserve the standard keywords and reply to them at the
network edge:

- **Twilio** automatically handles `STOP, STOPALL, STOP ALL, UNSUBSCRIBE, CANCEL,
  END, QUIT`. For these, Twilio sends its own STOP confirmation and blocks EzyText's
  duplicate reply. Your custom keywords (like `leave`) are detected and answered by
  EzyText instead.
- **Telnyx** behaves similarly when advanced opt-out sync is applied on the profile.

<Note>
  Because of this, your custom auto-reply may not be sent for the standard keywords
  — the provider's own STOP reply is delivered instead. Both may arrive in some
  configurations.
</Note>

## FAQ

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Does opting out apply across all channels?">
    Opting out flags the **contact**, so they're excluded from future SMS campaign
    audiences. Always confirm consent before re-adding anyone.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Can an opted-out contact rejoin?">
    Yes — if they reply with an opt-in keyword like **START**. See
    [Opt-in & auto-reply](/compliance/opt-in). They also can't be re-onboarded
    through a [join link](/campaigns/join-links) while opted out.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Is there a limit on keywords?">
    Yes, **75 per workspace**. Keywords are stored case-insensitively and must
    contain at least one letter or digit.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Where can I see who opted out?">
    Each contact's activity timeline shows an **Opted out** event with the keyword
    and inbound message. Opted-out contacts are excluded from campaign sends
    automatically.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Can I add a STOP footer to my messages?">
    EzyText doesn't add an opt-out footer automatically. Include opt-out language in
    your message copy where appropriate — carriers expect it, especially for
    marketing.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## Troubleshooting

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="My custom opt-out reply isn't being sent">
    For provider-reserved keywords (STOP, UNSUBSCRIBE, etc.), the carrier sends its
    own reply and blocks EzyText's. Your custom auto-reply is sent only for
    non-reserved keywords you've added yourself.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="A contact still received a campaign after opting out">
    Opt-out is evaluated at send time. If the campaign was already in flight when
    they replied STOP, the last message may have been queued. New campaigns exclude
    them. Confirm the **Opted out** event appears on their timeline.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="'STOP' matched but a longer word shouldn't have">
    Matching is whole-word and start-anchored, so `STOPOVER` won't match `STOP`. If
    a message opted out unexpectedly, check whether it *starts* with one of your
    keywords.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>
