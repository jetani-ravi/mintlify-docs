## Overview

EzyText accounts use email and password. This page covers signing up, logging in,
and recovering a lost password.

## Create an account

<Steps>
  <Step title="Register">
    Go to the sign-up page and enter your **First Name**, **Email**, and a
    **Password** (at least 8 characters), then confirm the password. Last name and
    phone are optional.
  </Step>
  <Step title="You're in">
    Your account is active immediately — there's no email verification step. A
    personal workspace is created for you automatically.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Note>
  If you're joining an existing team, use the **invitation link** from your invite
  email instead — it pre-fills your email and drops you straight into that
  workspace. See [Invite your team](/getting-started/invite-team).
</Note>

## Log in

Enter your email and password at the login screen. You'll stay signed in across
reloads — sessions refresh silently for up to a week before you need to log in
again.

## Reset your password

<Steps>
  <Step title="Request a reset">
    On the login screen, click **Forgot password?** and enter your email. If an
    account exists, a reset link is sent (the message is the same either way, to
    protect your privacy).
  </Step>
  <Step title="Set a new password">
    Open the emailed link and choose a new password. Reset passwords must be at
    least 8 characters and include an uppercase letter, a number, and a special
    character. The link is valid for **1 hour**.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## FAQ

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Is there two-factor authentication?">
    Account login uses email and password today. (SMS one-time codes are used for
    public join-link phone verification, not for logging in.)
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Why is the reset password stricter than sign-up?">
    Password resets enforce a stronger policy (uppercase, number, special character)
    as an extra safeguard when recovering access.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="I didn't get a reset email">
    Check spam, confirm you used the right address, and note the link expires after
    an hour — request a fresh one if needed.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## Troubleshooting

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="'Reset link expired'">
    The link is older than an hour, or a newer reset request replaced it. Request a
    new link from **Forgot password?**.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Can't log in after being invited">
    Make sure you accepted the invitation first. If your account isn't part of any
    workspace, ask an admin to re-invite you.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>
