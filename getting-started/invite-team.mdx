## Overview

Invite colleagues to collaborate in your workspace. Each member gets a
[role](/getting-started/users-roles) that controls what they can do. Manage
members and invitations at **Settings → Users** (`/settings/users`).

## Invite members

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open Users">
    Go to **Settings → Users** and click **Invite member**.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Add people">
    In the invite drawer, enter each person's **Full Name**, **Email**, and **Role**,
    and add them to the invitation list. You can queue several at once and add an
    optional **personal message**.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Send">
    Click **Send invitations**. Each person receives an email link to join.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Note>
  The **Owner** role can't be assigned via invitation. Invitations expire
  automatically after **72 hours**.
</Note>

## What the invitee does

When someone opens their invite link:

- **New to EzyText** — they're taken to sign-up with their email pre-filled, and
  join your workspace on account creation.
- **Existing user** — they see a "Join {workspace}" card and click **Accept** (or
  **Decline**).

## Manage invitations

On the **Invitations** tab you can **Resend** or **Revoke** a pending invite.
Invitation statuses are **Pending**, **Accepted**, **Declined**, **Revoked**, and
**Expired**.

## Manage members

On the **Members** tab, **Change role** or **Remove** a member. See
[Users & roles](/getting-started/users-roles) for what each role can do.

## FAQ

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="How many people can I invite?">
    Team size may be limited by your plan's seat entitlement. If you hit the limit,
    upgrade your [plan](/billing/wallet-billing).
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="An invite expired — what now?">
    Resend it from the Invitations tab. Pending invites auto-expire after 72 hours.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Can I invite someone as Owner?">
    No — the Owner role can't be invited or assigned. Assign Admin instead, or
    transfer ownership through support if needed.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>
